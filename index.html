2019.08.15 Highlight Reel: Twenty-Word Stories — Revolution

Twenty-word stories, to the theme of Revolution. From the session on 15 October.

  • They set her on fire. Burned her at the stake, shouting, “revolution!” She laughed as they laid her corpse down.
  • Men and women took up arms. We fought fierce battles with the loyalists. Alas, our blood was shed in vain.
  • They really wanted to start a revolution, poured their hearts and souls into it. It’s a shame they were ants.

Thanks to our lovely members for contributing these entries.

2019.08.15 Weekly Update #2: The Enemy of Art is the Absence of Limitations

That's Orson Welles, or something like that.

Yesterday's session was about writing under restrictions. A huge thanks to everyone who participated in the twenty-word-story challenge, to the theme of 'revolution'. We hope you enjoyed it! We loved how varied the responses were.

The take-home challenge this week is to put together a lipogram, where the use of the letters O and S is forbidden. We can't wait to hear what people come up with.

Our next meeting will be at 6:30PM on Tuesday, 22 October, in SAF 121. See you there!

2019.08.08 Weekly Update #1: Welcome (Back)

Thank you all so much for coming to the first Writers’ Society meeting of the term. We hope you enjoyed the session (and the tea & biscuits), had fun with the writing, and put plenty of words down on your text-processing medium of choice.

If you’ve gone home and written even more words, or done some editing, or if you have something else entirely you would like to share ⁠— remember that you are welcome to read your piece at the beginning of the next session!

Our next meeting will be at 6:30PM on Tuesday, 15 October, in SAF 121. We look forward to seeing you there!
